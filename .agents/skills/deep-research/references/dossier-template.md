# Research Log & Dossier Templates

Part A is the working log — copy it to `RESEARCH_LOG.md` before the first search and fill
it append-only as you work. Part B is the final deliverable structure. Nothing enters
Part B that isn't backed by a Part A ledger row.

---

## Part A · Working log (fill as you go)

### Question definition

```
QUESTION(S):          [ exactly what must be answered ]
SUFFICIENT ANSWER:    [ what "done" looks like — decisions this will drive ]
FRESHNESS REQUIRED:   [ must be current as of ___ / historical is fine ]
KNOWN UNKNOWNS:       [ what we already know we don't know ]
OUT OF SCOPE:         [ explicitly not being researched ]
DATE STARTED:         [ YYYY-MM-DD ]
```

## Part B · Final dossier structure

```
# [Title — the question, as a statement]
Researched: [YYYY-MM-DD] · Shelf life: [when this needs rechecking, and which parts]

## 1 · Direct answer
[ The answer in 2–5 sentences, leading with the conclusion. Confidence: HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW
  and why. If the honest answer is "partially determined" or "disputed", say so here,
  not in a footnote. ]

## 2 · Key findings (ranked by confidence)
[ Each finding: one bold claim sentence → supporting detail → citations [S1][S2] →
  grade (VERIFIED / SINGLE-SOURCE). Findings the reader would act on come first.
  FACT, INFERENCE, and SPECULATION are labeled — never blended. Inferences say
  "based on [S1] and [S3], I infer…". ]

## 3 · Disputed & conflicting
[ Where credible sources disagree: both positions, both citations, and your assessment
  (labeled as inference) of which is better supported and why. ]

## 4 · Gaps — what could not be verified
[ Each gap: what's missing, why it matters, where you looked (from the search trail),
  and the most promising lead for whoever picks this up. NONE only if truly none. ]

## 5 · Detail sections
[ Optional: per-subtopic depth for readers who need more than §2. Same citation rules. ]

## 6 · Sources
[ Numbered. For each: [S1] Title — author/org — URL — published date — accessed date —
  tier — one-line note on why it's trustworthy (or its bias). ]
```

### Compilation rules

- **Answer first.** The reader gets the conclusion before the evidence, always.
- **Every fact carries its citation inline** — a bibliography alone is not traceability.
- **Write for the reader in six months:** absolute dates ("as of July 2026"), never
  "currently" or "recently"; spell out acronyms once; no reference to "the search above".
- **Confidence is visible everywhere** — a dossier that looks uniformly certain is lying
  about something.
- **Shorter and verified beats longer and padded.** Cut any sentence that exists to make
  the research look bigger.
